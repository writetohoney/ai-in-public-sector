# Consolidated reading list

A single-page index of the course readings, grouped by session and mapped to the [adaptive capacity diagnostic](./adaptive-capacity-diagnostic.md).

**Links live in [`syllabus.md`](../syllabus.md), not here.** This is a navigation aid; keeping the URLs in one place means there is only one thing to maintain and nothing to drift out of sync.

Because AI moves quickly, the 2025–2026 items — especially the comparative-governance cases — should be refreshed each time the course is taught.

---

## Books

- *Recoding America* — Jennifer Pahlka (2023) — **start here** for the public-sector lens
- *Does Technology Drive History? The Dilemma of Technological Determinism* — Merritt Roe Smith & Leo Marx, eds. (1994) — the founding question
- *Co-Intelligence* — Ethan Mollick (2024)
- *The Alignment Problem* — Brian Christian (2020)
- *Power and Prediction* — Agrawal, Gans & Goldfarb (2022)
- *A Civic Technologist's Practice Guide* — Cyd Harrell (2020)
- *Empire of AI* — Karen Hao (2025)

## Session 1 — Why this, why now; is any of it inevitable?

- Does Technology Drive History? The Dilemma of Technological Determinism — Smith & Marx, eds. (1994). Key essays: Smith, "Technological Determinism in American Culture"; Heilbroner, "Do Machines Make History?"; Bimber, "Three Faces of Technological Determinism"; Hughes, "Technological Momentum"; Misa, "Retrieving Sociotechnical Change from Technological Determinism"
- GenAI for Everyone — DeepLearning.AI
- Implementing AI in the Public Sector — Mergel, Dickinson, Stenvall & Gasco (2024)
- Digital transformation toward AI-augmented public administration — Ahn & Chen (2022)
- Your jurisdiction's national or state AI strategy (e.g. Canada's GC AI Strategy)

## Session 2 — How AI works (fluency)

- Large Language Models, explained — Lee & Trott
- What is Machine Learning? — Apolitical (video)
- Attention Is All You Need — Vaswani et al. (2017) — *optional, technical*
