# Final Project — Detailed Policy Proposal and/or AI Policy Agent (25%) + Oral Presentation (25%)

**Format:** groups of 3–4 · **Deliverable due:** before Session 14 · **Presentation:** Session 14

Choose **one** track. Both must confront the governance tensions your use case raises.

---

## Track A — Policy Intervention Memo (~4,000 words)

A memo addressing a specific AI governance or deployment challenge in the public sector. Include:

- **Problem definition**
- **Stakeholder analysis**
- **Options analysis** — at least three options
- **Recommended course of action**
- **Implementation roadmap**
- **Risk assessment**
- **Tensions and trade-offs** — an explicit section on what your recommendation gains, what it
  risks, and who bears the cost

---

## Track B — AI Agent or Tool Prototype

A functional AI agent or tool prototype for a public-sector use case, plus a **~2,000-word design
rationale** covering:

- the problem addressed and who it serves
- the technical approach
- **governance considerations** — including a responsible-use and risk assessment, accountability,
  and contestability (see the [`ai-policy-agents/`](../ai-policy-agents) governance note)
- the limitations

Place your prototype in its own folder under [`ai-policy-agents/`](../ai-policy-agents), using the
[`example-agent/`](../ai-policy-agents/example-agent) template.

---

## Oral Presentation (25%)

A 15–20 minute group presentation of your proposal or prototype to the class and an invited panel,
with Q&A.

## Graded on

**Deliverable:** depth of analysis or technical sophistication; feasibility and realism;
integration of course concepts; quality of writing or technical execution; and how seriously the
governance tensions are confronted.

**Presentation:** clarity and structure; quality of argument and evidence; ability to handle Q&A;
professional delivery and time management.
