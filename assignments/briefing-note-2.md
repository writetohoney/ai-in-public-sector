# Assignment 2 — Briefing Note 2 (20%)

**Length:** ~2,500 words · **Format:** individual · **Due:** after Session 9

## The task

Choose **one** of two routes.

**Option A — Topic.** Write a briefing note on one or more topics from Sessions 4–9: procurement
and the machinery of government, building and modernizing public-sector AI, data and algorithmic
harm, the human in the loop, or responsible AI.

**Option B — Institution.** Write a briefing note to a senior decision-maker in the institution
you have been assessing, on the dimension of adaptive capacity where you judge it weakest so far.
By this point you will have completed five diagnostic entries (Sessions 3, 4, 5, 8, and 9) — this
option turns that evidence into advice someone could act on. Observe the guardrails on assessing
your own institution set out in the syllabus: nothing confidential, and no naming of individuals.

Either route should go beyond description to **analysis** and **concrete recommendations**.

Engage substantively with **at least two course readings**, and apply an explicit **tensions
lens**: name the central trade-off your topic or institution forces — what is gained, what is
risked, and who bears the cost — and let that shape your recommendations. Your
[tensions ledger](../resources/tensions-ledger-template.md) and
[diagnostic](../resources/adaptive-capacity-diagnostic.md) entries are the raw material for this.

## What to include

- A clear problem statement and why it matters — for the public sector (Option A) or for your institution (Option B)
- Substantive engagement with at least two course readings and, where useful, external sources
- An explicit treatment of the governing tension(s) at stake
- Realistic, actionable recommendations

## Using AI

As with Briefing Note 1: AI is permitted as a drafting aid, must be cited, apply the course
custom instructions, append an interaction log, and verify everything yourself.

## Graded on

- Quality of argument and analysis
- Engagement with course materials and external sources
- How well the central trade-off is surfaced and handled
- Realistic, actionable recommendations
